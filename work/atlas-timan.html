Project Engineer

ATLAS · TIMAN Group, UIUC - an adaptive reskilling platform that assembles an “elastic textbook” on demand.

What I did

  • Developing an adaptive reskilling platform that assembles an elastic textbook on demand, synthesizing personalized learning material from research literature to match each learner’s existing mastery and immediate work context.
  • Contributing to a six-stage LLM pipeline - PDF parsing into structured information units, CiTO-aligned citation-relation typing, and evidence-traceable “PaperCard” extraction organized by Bloom’s taxonomy.
  • Building a prerequisite knowledge graph that pairs BFS expansion with a greedy set-cover algorithm to generate phased, mastery-personalized reading lists.
  • The work targets a November 2026 milestone tied to a $10M funding opportunity (Laude Competition).

Context

ATLAS sits in the TIMAN group under Prof. ChengXiang Zhai, and the problem it takes on is a real gap rather than an academic one. New capability appears in research literature years before it appears in anything teachable. A paper is where the knowledge lives; a paper is also close to the worst possible artifact to learn from if you do not already have the background it assumes.

Reskilling platforms usually respond to that by fixing a curriculum and hoping it matches the learner. ATLAS inverts it: the material is assembled per learner, from the literature, against what they already know and what they are trying to do this week. That is what the word elastic is carrying - the textbook is not selected, it is generated, and it is a different textbook for a different person.

What it has taught me so far

Personalization is a graph problem before it is a generation problem. The instinct is to reach for the model - given a topic and a learner, write the lesson. But the hard question comes earlier and it is structural: what must this person already understand before this paper is readable at all? That is a prerequisite relation over a graph, and once you have it, choosing the shortest set of readings that covers the gaps is a set-cover problem with a known greedy answer. The LLM writes the material; the graph decides what material is worth writing.

Traceability is a design constraint, not a feature. Nothing about an educational system survives a confident invented citation. Structuring extractions as evidence-linked PaperCards means every claim the platform teaches can be walked back to the passage it came from - which constrains the pipeline everywhere upstream, and is exactly why it is six stages rather than one prompt.
