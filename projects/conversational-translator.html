Conversational Translator

Full-duplex speech-to-speech translation - one persistent bidirectional stream instead of a request/response round trip.

Summary

  • Architected a full-duplex translator, chaining Google Speech-to-Text for input, the Gemini API for translation and Text-to-Speech for audible output into a single real-time pipeline.
  • Engineered the communication layer with gRPC, holding a persistent bidirectional stream between client and server for a natural conversational flow at significantly lower latency than a REST equivalent.

The problem

Conversation has a latency budget, and it is short. Past roughly a second of delay people stop talking to each other and start taking turns at a machine, which is a different and much worse experience.

Three cloud services in series is exactly how you spend that budget. Worse, request/response makes the spending structural: with REST you record an utterance, then upload it, then wait, then translate, then wait, then synthesise. Nothing overlaps, and the user pays for every stage twice - once in processing and once in the round trip that carries it.

How it works

The client opens one bidirectional gRPC stream and keeps it. Audio frames go up as they are captured rather than after the sentence ends, so recognition is already running while the speaker is still talking. Interim transcripts come back continuously; a finalised segment is what triggers translation, and the translated text goes straight to synthesis with the resulting audio flowing back down the same open connection.

gRPC is doing real work here and not just being a fashionable transport. One connection, negotiated once, carrying four legs of traffic in both directions with typed messages: the alternative is four HTTP exchanges per utterance, each re-paying setup cost, and a client that has to reassemble ordering itself.

What I’d change

The pipeline is still serial where it counts. Streaming removes the transport waits but recognition, translation and synthesis still happen strictly in order, so the floor is the sum of three stages. Speculative translation of interim transcripts - starting on a partial and correcting if the final differs - is the obvious next move and the obvious next source of bugs.

There is also an unavoidable trade between quality and immediacy. A longer segment gives the model more context and a better translation, and costs exactly that much conversational time. That is a product decision wearing an engineering costume, and I settled it by feel rather than by testing it on people.
